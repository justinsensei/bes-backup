# ADHD Product Design Concepts & Opportunities

This reference captures a synthesized set of core design principles and product opportunity spaces identified during the June 2026 audit of ADHD planners, learning games, and clinical attention tools.

## 1. Resilience Over Consistency
For neurodivergent users, strict routine adherence is incredibly difficult. Traditional engagement mechanics (daily streaks, penalties, or rigid backlogs) trigger an all-or-nothing "streak-broken shame loop," leading to immediate abandonment.
*   **Flipped Gamification:** Reward cumulative progress (e.g., total days active) instead of consecutive streaks.
*   **Compassionate Re-entry:** Eliminate red badges or "overdue" shame indicators upon a user's return. Use low-friction greetings ("Welcome back! Let's get a quick win today").

## 2. Context Restoration ("Session Save")
Context-switching is roughly 50% more expensive for individuals with ADHD. Users resort to "tab hoarding" as externalized working memory, which inevitably collapses into tab anxiety and avoidance.
*   **The "Session Save" Mechanic:** Treat partial progress as a first-class state. Auto-save precise layouts, open tabs, and draft states.
*   **Micro-Recaps:** Upon return, present a 1-sentence recap of what the user was doing and the single next physical step required.

## 3. Regulation-Aware Scheduling
Traditional planners assume a flat, linear supply of executive function. ADHD users experience highly spiky executive energy tied to emotional regulation and biochem/medication cycles.
*   **Dynamic Throttling:** When a user is depleted, automatically collapse complex timelines or lists into a highly opinionated, single-choice layout.
*   **Emotional Planning Inputs:** Design systems that anticipate energy dips (medication crash zones) and match task complexity to the user's emotional state.

## 4. Interest-Coupled Instruction
ADHD brains are fueled by high intrinsic motivation, novelty, and intense interest (hyperfocus). Rigid linear curricula trigger cognitive avoidance.
*   **Curriculum Wrapping:** Leverage LLMs to wrap core learning concepts (languages, math) in the user's current hyperfocus topic (e.g., skateboarding, fantasy novels).
*   **Hyperfocus Detection:** If a user is highly engaged, suspend session/time limits and dynamically offer deeper, non-linear lessons rather than interrupting flow.

## 5. School-to-Home Coordination Fabric
Traditional student planners require high executive overhead to maintain, leading to the "app graveyard" phenomenon.
*   **LMS Bridges:** Automate the ingestion of school LMS notifications (Canvas, Google Classroom) to bridge the gap between "assignment posted" and "actually done" without parent or student data entry overhead.
*   **Multi-Actor UX:** Provide kids with on-rails, next-step views while offering parents a low-friction administrative scaffolding view.
